American Heart Association recommends:

  • a wide variety of fruits and vegetables

  • whole grains and products made up of mostly grains

  • healthy sources of protein (mostly plants such as legumes and nuts; fish and seafood; low-fat or nonfat dairy; and, if you eat meat and poultry, ensuring it is lean and unprocessed)

  • liquid non-tropical vegetable oils

  • minimally processed foods

  • minimized intake of added sugars

  • foods prepared with little or no salt

  • limited or preferably no alcohol intake


American Heart Association recommends you avoid:

  1. Saturated Fats: Can raise blood cholesterol levels.
    • Fatty cuts of meat (like beef, lamb, pork)
    • Chicken with the skin
    • Whole-fat dairy products (like butter, cheese, milk)
    • Tropical oils (like coconut oil, palm oil)
  2. Trans Fats: Raise bad cholesterol and lower good cholesterol.
    • Processed foods
    • Commercially baked goods (like cookies, cakes)
    • Anything with "partially hydrogenated oils" in the ingredients
  3. Sodium (Salt): Can contribute to high blood pressure.
    • Processed foods
    • Canned soups
    • Some breads and rolls
    • Cold cuts and cured meats
    • Poultry
    • Pizza
  4. Sugary Foods and Beverages: Can lead to weight gain and other health issues.
    • Soft drinks
    • Candy
    • Baked goods
    • Many packaged foods
  5. Red Meat: Limit and opt for alternative protein sources like:
    • Fish
    • Poultry
    • Plant-based proteins
  6. Alcohol: If consumed, it should be in moderation.
    • Up to one drink per day for women
    • Up to two drinks per day for men
Plant-Based Proteins
  1. Legumes:
    • Lentils
    • Chickpeas (garbanzo beans)
    • Black beans
    • Kidney beans
    • Pinto beans
    • Mung beans
    • Peas
  2. Grains:
    • Quinoa
    • Farro
    • Bulgur
    • Barley
    • Brown rice
    • Oats
  3. Nuts and Seeds:
    • Almonds
    • Peanuts
    • Walnuts
    • Chia seeds
    • Flaxseeds
    • Hemp seeds
    • Sunflower seeds
    • Pumpkin seeds (pepitas)
    • Sesame seeds
  4. Soy Products:
    • Tofu
    • Tempeh
    • Edamame (young soybeans)
    • Textured vegetable protein (TVP)
    • Soy milk
  5. Seitan: Also known as wheat meat or wheat gluten, it's made from gluten, the main protein in wheat.
  6. Plant-Based Meat Alternatives: Products that mimic the taste and texture of meat, made from various plant-based ingredients like Beyond Meat, Impossible Foods, etc.
  7. Green Vegetables:
    • Broccoli
    • Spinach
    • Brussels sprouts
    • Asparagus
    • Green beans
  8. Mycoprotein: Found in products like Quorn, it's a protein-rich fungus-based source.
  9. Spirulina: A blue-green algae that's a complete protein source.
  10. Nutritional Yeast: Often used for its cheesy flavor in vegan dishes, it's also a good source of protein.
List of Fruits and Their Heart Health Benefits
  • Berries (Blueberries, Strawberries, Raspberries, Blackberries): Rich in antioxidants, particularly flavonoids, which have been associated with improved heart health.
  • Citrus Fruits (Oranges, Grapefruits, Lemons, Limes): High in vitamin C and other antioxidants. They contain flavonoids, which may help to lower blood pressure and reduce inflammation.
  • Apples: Great source of fiber and vitamin C, both of which have been shown to help lower the risk of heart disease.
  • Avocado: Rich in monounsaturated fats, which can help to reduce bad cholesterol levels, lower blood pressure, and improve heart health.
  • Pomegranate: Packed with antioxidants, such as anthocyanins and polyphenols, that have been shown to improve cholesterol profiles and lower blood pressure.
  • Bananas: Good source of potassium, which helps to control blood pressure.
List of Legumes and Their Heart Health Benefits
  • Lentils: Great source of protein and fiber, which can help lower cholesterol and stabilize blood sugar levels.
  • Chickpeas (Garbanzo Beans): Rich in fiber and protein, and have been shown to help reduce levels of LDL (“bad”) cholesterol.
  • Black Beans: Good source of fiber, protein, and antioxidants that help to lower inflammation in the body and improve heart health.
  • Kidney Beans: Great source of fiber and protein, which help to lower cholesterol and stabilize blood sugar levels.
  • Navy Beans: High in fiber and protein, and have been found to help reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease.
  • Pinto Beans: Good source of fiber and protein, and have been shown to help lower cholesterol levels.
  • Soybeans (and Soy Products): Good source of protein and are low in saturated fat. Some research suggests that consuming soy products may help to reduce LDL cholesterol levels and improve heart health.
  • Peas (including Split Peas): Low in fat and also a good source of fiber and protein, which makes them beneficial for heart health.
List of Heart-Healthy Vegetables and Their Benefits
  • Leafy Greens (Spinach, Kale, Collard Greens): High in fiber, antioxidants, and nitrates, which can help to lower blood pressure.
  • Tomatoes: Rich in lycopene, a powerful antioxidant that has been associated with a reduced risk of heart disease.
  • Carrots: High in fiber and antioxidants like beta-carotene, which are good for your heart.
  • Beets: High in nitrates, which can help to lower blood pressure. They are also a good source of antioxidants and fiber.
  • Broccoli and Other Cruciferous Vegetables (Cauliflower, Cabbage, Brussels Sprouts): High in fiber and contain antioxidants that may help to reduce inflammation and improve heart health.
  • Legumes (Beans, Lentils, Chickpeas): Great source of protein and fiber, which can help to lower cholesterol and improve heart health.
  • Sweet Potatoes: Great source of fiber, vitamins, and antioxidants like beta-carotene, which are good for your heart.
  • Red, Yellow, and Orange Peppers: High in vitamin C and other antioxidants, which are good for heart health.
